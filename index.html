FAQs

Got questions? We've got answers.

Still stuck? Reach out to our team directly — we reply within 24 hours.

Is there any registration fee, participation fee or hidden charge?

No. The Bharat Innovation Challenge is completely free. There are no registration, participation or hidden charges for students, mentors or schools at any stage of the competition.

Do we need coding knowledge or a working prototype to participate in Stage 1?

No. Students from all backgrounds can participate. Stage 1 accepts ideas, wireframes, mockups, sketches and concept presentations. Teams can use no-code AI tools to prototype. A working prototype is encouraged but not mandatory at Stage 1.

Why should students participate? What makes BIC different?

BIC is more than a competition. Students build real solutions, pitch before industry experts, showcase prototypes and compete for ₹16L+ across 18 award categories on a national platform.

Besides prize money, what opportunities can students expect?

Beyond prizes, students gain mentorship, networking, media exposure and internship opportunities. Outstanding teams may also receive direct interest from industry leaders and investors.

How many students can be in a team, and can students from different schools join together?

Students of Classes 8–12 (ages 13–18) from any Indian school board can participate. Teams can have 1–5 students and one mentor. Cross-school teams are welcome.

What happens after the Stage 1 results are out?

Shortlisted teams are invited to LPU's campus at Phagwara for the Grand Finale. LPU provides accommodation, meals and local transportation. Finalists must bring their working prototype, refined pitch deck and display materials for the Expo.

Will all participants receive certificates?

Yes. Certificates are issued to all participants, and same-day hardcopy certificates are issued to all finalists at the Grand Finale. Mentors, finalists and winners all receive official recognition.

How are ideas evaluated and what determines the winners?

Every submission gets 100% human evaluation. Projects are judged by founders, investors, corporate leaders and academicians on innovation, feasibility, impact, presentation quality, execution and future potential.

Official declaration, undertaking & terms of participation

Submission is final
Once submitted, the PPT/PDF cannot be edited, replaced or withdrawn during evaluation. Shortlisted teams must confirm participation, and withdrawal after confirmation forfeits the team's slot.
Originality & fair use
Projects, ideas and presentations must be original. Plagiarism, false claims, copied work or AI-generated presentations without genuine innovation will result in immediate and permanent disqualification. The core idea cannot be changed after submission; only refinements are permitted.
Team composition
Each team must consist of 1–5 students and one mentor. Any change in team members or mentor requires prior approval from the Organising Committee. A parent may act as mentor only with official school validation.
Submission format
Only PPT or PDF submissions (maximum 15 slides) following the prescribed file naming convention will be accepted. Non-compliant submissions may be rejected without review.
Confirmation & shortlisting
Selection for the Grand Finale is based on Stage 1 evaluation. Participation is confirmed only after acceptance by the Organising Committee.
Travel
Complimentary pick-up and drop are available only from designated locations (Amritsar Airport, Jalandhar Junction and Phagwara Railway Station). Travel to these pickup points and any additional expenses are the participant's responsibility. No travel reimbursement will be provided.
Accommodation & meals
Complimentary accommodation and meals are provided only for registered students and one mentor per team during the Grand Finale. Separate hostel arrangements for boys and girls are mandatory. Parents and guardians must arrange their own stay, meals and transportation.
Identity verification
A valid school ID and government photo ID (Aadhaar/PAN/Passport) are mandatory throughout the event. Official BIC ID cards must be worn on campus. Entry may be denied without valid identification.
Dress code
School uniform or formal attire is mandatory during all official sessions. Casual wear, ripped jeans, shorts, slippers and flip-flops are not permitted.
Code of conduct
Participants are expected to maintain professional behaviour throughout the competition. Harassment, bullying, discrimination, misconduct or any inappropriate behaviour will lead to immediate disqualification and removal from the event.
Strictly prohibited

Jury decisions
All jury decisions are final and binding. No appeals, negotiations or requests for re-evaluation will be entertained. Any attempt to influence the judging process will result in immediate disqualification.
Attendance
Attendance at all official sessions, mentor interactions, exhibition hours, judging rounds and the Award Ceremony is compulsory. Late reporting or absence may affect evaluation or lead to disqualification.
Medical declaration
Participants must disclose any pre-existing medical conditions before arrival. Basic first aid will be available on campus; hospitalisation and related medical expenses remain the participant's responsibility.
Media consent
By participating, teams grant LPU and the Bharat Innovation Challenge permission to use project details, photographs, videos, names and event content for promotional, educational and media purposes without additional consent or compensation.
