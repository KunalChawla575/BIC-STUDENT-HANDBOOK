FAQs

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common questions.

Is there any registration fee, participation fee or hidden charge at any stage of the competition?

No. The Bharat Innovation Challenge is completely free. There are no registration, participation or hidden charges for students, mentors or schools at any stage of the competition.

Do we need coding knowledge, technical skills or a working prototype to participate in Stage 1?

No. Students from all backgrounds can participate. Stage 1 accepts ideas, wireframes, mockups, sketches and concept presentations. A working prototype is encouraged but not mandatory.

Why should students participate? What makes BIC different from other competitions?

BIC is more than a competition. Students build real solutions, pitch before industry experts, showcase prototypes and compete for ₹16L+ across 18 award categories on a national platform.

Besides prize money, what opportunities can students expect?

Beyond prizes, students gain mentorship, networking, media exposure and internship opportunities. Outstanding teams may also receive direct interest from industry leaders and investors.

How many students can be in a team, and can students from different schools participate together?

Students of Classes 8–12 (ages 13–18) from any Indian school board can participate. Teams can have 1–5 students and one mentor. Cross-school teams are welcome.

What facilities are provided by LPU, and what should finalists bring?

LPU provides accommodation, meals and local transportation, while finalists must bring their working prototype, refined pitch deck and display materials for the Expo.

Will we receive certificates, awards or recognition for participating?

Yes. Mentors, finalists and winners receive official recognition based on their journey in the competition. Finalists also gain national exposure through the Innovation Expo and Grand Finale.

How are ideas evaluated, who are the jury members and what determines the winners?

Projects are evaluated by founders, investors, corporate leaders and academicians based on innovation, feasibility, impact, presentation quality, execution and future potential.
